From Love

From love is born obligation. When he was but a child, my fathers wanted to be free, to travel the world; he was a pirate of a man. He wanted to steal adventure, the hearts of woman, perhaps even life itself. In a hollywood movie such a mans would be the hero of tales; but in reality most of these types of man are blind; never realizing that everyone else also thinks themself to be the hero. Funny thought yes? Afteralls, if everyone was a hero, why is the world not at peace? We know the answer don't we: because most people are not the heroes they desires to be, and the world probably doesnt deserve the heroes of the movies. What we do deserve may be something else entirley.

And so this is the story. When my father was a pirate is in his late 20s, he tried to steal the heart of a woman who was a nurse. She was not stolen--too smart to be--but a woman may be at times in her hauty natures, open to short relationships even with those maybe not best suited for their well being, and such was this case.

They snuck around their relationship, but the doctor eventual learned of his fiances' affair and this woman lost her engagement with the doctors.

'You must take responsibility', the woman told me father, 'Those in our circles know of our affair and speak ills about me. Marry me instead of the doctor to right our wrongs and decisions.'; but alas, my father was nothing but a pirate afterall, and he left on the seas of irresponsibility leaving the woman alone... or so he thought? What my fathers did not know, was that he hads left the woman with a parting gift; the essence of a child within her.

Now you can maybe guess the secret before I even tell you, yes? The nurse was my mother, and I was the unborns child within her. When my mother learn of her pregnancy she wrote the letters across the world, sent the message through the telephone lines to find this pirate of a man... but there was no answers. This man seemed to stop being a pirate; more like a ghost he seemed.

'There is no way this man, through all my hard work of findings him, did not at least receives one message of the news of my pregnancy', my mother thoughts. So she resign herself to the reality; yes, this man knew of his unborn child but it was just simple harsh truth; this pirate of a man did not care.

So it was that my mother got larger and larger each months, pregnant with childs and no husband beside her. There were manys who looked down at her: 'This promoscious woman have child... and with whom?' There was no one my mother could say who it was, so the whispers... they talked, and they grew and grew. Yes, of course, the people and whispers said; my mother was 'promiscuous', a 'cheat', 'no good'. They felt vitriols for the woman, and pity for me, the unborn child of a bastard relationships.

And then one day it happen; the pain she think was regular turn out not to be so regular... and on a January day I come into the world; actually months before anyones could have expected; so earlyer than expected due date that in facts that it was too dangerous for both myself and my mothers.

I start this story, and I say the words: 'From love is born obligation.' This is not actually my words; this is my fathers. This is the words he say to my mother when he walk into the hospital room, and the doctor say there's a chance she cannot make it through the next week. 'I loved my son when I first heard of him', he had told her. 'And from love is born obligation'. This is the translation of his words to my dying mother.

This pirate of a man, who tried to steal life away, perhaps had his own stolen away by the circumstances; as he would tell me as i grew up, 'But such is the fate of those who love others as I loved you', he would often said to me during bedtime story, 'And such is the fate of a man who learned to love your mother.'

This is the story of how a person like me came to be; an obligation who formed love, solidified through a magic spell. It is not a fairy tale you see in the movies, and there is no hero in the ways hollywood can write; all the characters are quites flawed, no? but perhaps our ideas of 'heroes' do not come in the form of people.
